Amazon Rainforest Still On Fire

#4G7487

The Amazon rainforest had a severe forest fire in the year 2019. The Governments of the South-American Countries had taken huge steps to stop it. At that time, it was a trending topic getting viral globally. But now in 2021, when the Amazon is burning again, no one is talking about it. The Amazon Rainforest is considered to be the lungs of the Earth as it provides most of the oxygen to the mass population. If this forest continues burning, sooner the oxygen level would deplete and people may suffer from breathing problems like bronchitis and Asthma. Brazil covers most of the Amazon Rainforest, the biggest Rainforest of the World, and the whole world is seeking aid to stop this burning. It is now told, that the rainforest, releasing most O2, is now releasing much more CO2, that is fatal for the whole biosphere. It is emitting more CO2 than it absorbs. As stated by a Geographic Explorer and Conservationist, that in certain areas, the burning rate is so rapid, that in every minute, a part equivalent to 3 soccer fields are getting burned. Not only CO2, but also CO (Carbon Monoxide) is also released due to incomplete combustion taking place at a mass amount, which gas is fatal for mankind ; leads to severe health issue and even death may occur. This may also lead to Species Extinction, which is already discussed.It is specified that at least 40,000 distinguished species are at risk.
