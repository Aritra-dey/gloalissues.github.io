Illegal Immigration

#546948

The Government highly discourages the practice of illegal immigration and too has legal process of migration, but under the table, some illegal practices of migration takes place which the government condemns and opposes.

One of the Largest Migration of the world, Indo-Pak Migration of 1947 reported 1 million official deaths and over 20 million were displaced and millions were killed and thousands were abused.
Currently, illegal migrants from Bangladesh has been a threat for India in terms of terrorism, contribution to overpopulation, etc.

The Central Government enacted CAA (Citizenship Amendment Act) and NRC (National Register of Citizens) and is in action but few provincial governments oppose it and severe riots caused within the country in early 2020. This was to ensure proper and legal registration of citizens but the border-state, West Bengal Govt., highly opposed it initially.

Illiteracy has a greater contribution in supporting such opposition, which will be discussed in the upcoming article.

In states like Assam, West Bengal, Tripura, Arunachal, Punjab - there is not only a high chance but also migration is practised. The government has witnessed that if it doesn’t enacts CAA, NRC soon, the situation may get worse than ever. A person irrespective of religion, caste, gender, race will get a legal identity but the government discourages an illegal approach made by certain migrants.

If this isn’t stopped further, threats like - terrorism, religious riots, religious threats and violence, extreme over-population, scarcity of resources due to overpopulation, etc. may hamper India and it's Development.

Water Logging and Flooding

#3T5984

Being surrounded by high Himalayan mountains at the north and INDIAN OCEAN, Bay of Bengal, Arabian Sea at the south, east and west respectively, India receives heavy rainfall except in certain areas. Not only the coastal states but the landlocked states too suffer from flooding and water-logging.
A Few Years ago, there was a tragic flood between the mountains of Kedarnath, a Hindu Holy Place in Uttar Pradesh, a landlocked province of India. In recent times too, areas of UP are being flooded every year. Every year bridges, roads, rails communication get isolated from rural areas and cause severe illness due to lack of supply of food, water, medicine, etc. Not only UP, but Bihar, West Bengal, Odisha, Meghalaya also suffer from this issue. At the South of WB and Odisha, every year, flooding takes place due to Cyclones.
Even in the cities like Kolkata and Bhubaneswar, water logging takes place. This water-logging has reported several deaths , even from Electric Shock. The historic houses are built in such a way, that if the roads get elevated, water will drain to the houses, creating more problems, and if not, a whole area may get isolated.

People suffer a lot, especially the poor and middle-class, due to water-logging even. Being settled at the bank of the Ganges, West and South Kolkata suffers more from water-logging. This has led to several diseases like Dengue, Malaria, Food-Poisoning and even death has occured.
If this is not stopped further, the situation may get worse.
But, now, the government has taken a few steps to prevent it ; like Elevation of Priority Roads, Proper Wiring of Electric Cables, evacuating people from houses prone to fire or that have been aged more than a century. The people are looking forward with the government to seek some more progress. The Govt. has also planned to save the water received during heavy rainfall in places like Cherrapunjee, Meghalaya, World’s wettest place which receives the heaviest rainfall to prevent Water-Scarcity in the country.
